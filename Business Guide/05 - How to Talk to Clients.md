# 05 — How to Talk to Clients

**Author:** Kutloano Moshao
**Date:** 2026

---

## The Golden Rule

Never talk about technology. Talk about business results.

**Wrong:** "I'll use React, Supabase, and Vercel with a PWA architecture."
**Right:** "Your guests will be able to browse your rooms and book
directly from their phones, even with bad signal."

Clients do not care how you build it. They care what it does for them.

---

## How to Present Your Service

### Opening Statement
> "I build custom websites for guest houses and small businesses in
> South Africa. What I build is not a template — it is a system built
> specifically for your business. Your staff can manage everything
> themselves through a simple admin panel, and your guests can browse
> and book from any device, even offline."

### When They Ask "What Does It Cost?"
Never give a price immediately. First ask questions:

1. "How many rooms do you have?"
2. "Do you have a restaurant or bar?"
3. "Do you currently have a website?"
4. "What is your biggest frustration with how you currently handle bookings?"
5. "What would make your life easier?"

Then say: "Based on what you've told me, I can put together a proposal
for you. Can we meet on [day] so I can show you what I've built for
other businesses?"

---

## Handling Price Objections

### "That's too expensive"
> "I understand. Let me put it in perspective — if your website brings
> in just one extra booking per month, that pays for 3 months of the
> retainer. The website works for you 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
> What does one booking earn you?"

### "Can't you just do it once-off, no monthly fee?"
> "I can do that, but I want to be honest with you — without ongoing
> maintenance, the website will start breaking within 6 months.
> Security vulnerabilities appear, certificates expire, integrations
> stop working. The monthly fee protects your investment and ensures
> everything keeps working. Think of it like a service plan on your car."

### "My nephew/friend can do it cheaper"
> "Absolutely, and I respect that. What I offer is professional-grade
> software with security, backups, and guaranteed support. When
> something breaks at 8pm on a Saturday before a busy long weekend,
> you'll have my number and I'll fix it. Can your nephew guarantee that?"

### "I'll think about it"
> "Of course, take your time. Can I ask — is there anything specific
> holding you back? I want to make sure I've answered all your
> questions before you decide."

### "The other guy quoted me R5,000"
> "There are R5,000 websites and there are R30,000 websites. The
> difference is what you get. A R5,000 website is usually a template
> with your logo on it. What I build is a custom system with an admin
> panel, security, offline capability, and ongoing support. Would you
> like me to show you the difference?"

## Presenting the Monthly Retainer

Never say "hosting fee" or "maintenance fee." Say:

> "The monthly retainer of R1,800 covers everything needed to keep
> your digital business running. This includes hosting, database,
> security monitoring, content updates, and technical support.
> Think of it as your digital office rent — it keeps everything
> running so you can focus on your guests."

---

## The Demo Close

Your best sales tool is showing your work. Always say:

> "Let me show you what I built for Green Acorn Guest House in
> Ficksburg. Go to greenacorn.co.za on your phone."

Then walk them through:
1. The home page and hero image
2. The rooms section
3. The gallery
4. The menu
5. Then say: "Now let me show you the admin panel where the owner
   manages everything herself."

After the demo: "I can build something like this for your business.
Customized for you, with your branding and your content."

---

## Red Flags — Clients to Avoid

Walk away from clients who:
- Refuse to pay a deposit
- Want unlimited revisions for a fixed price
- Say "it should only take you a few hours"
- Have already fired 2 other developers
- Want you to start before signing a contract
- Keep changing the scope without agreeing to extra charges
- Are rude or disrespectful in initial conversations

Your time is valuable. Bad clients cost you more than they pay you.

---

## Green Flags — Good Clients

Look for clients who:
- Ask good questions about the process
- Understand that quality takes time
- Pay the deposit without argument
- Provide content on time
- Respect your expertise
- Have a real business with real revenue

---

## Following Up

If a client says "I'll think about it":
- Follow up after 3 days: "Just checking in — do you have any questions?"
- Follow up after 1 week: "I wanted to share something relevant to your business."
- Follow up after 2 weeks: "I have a slot opening up next month if you'd like to proceed."
- After 3 follow-ups with no response: move on.

---

*The best salespeople listen more than they talk.*
*Ask questions. Understand the problem. Then offer the solution.*
